We developed a novel approach to predict joint angles in walking Drosophila using treadmill velocity data. By creating deep learning models and fine-tuning them on 600-frame kinematic trials, we have success in predicting joint angles during both walking and turning behaviors. This work provides a simple fly-on-treadmill setup to estimate kinematics without complex multi-camera systems, making it easier to correlate neural activity with leg movements. I recently presented on our progress at the Max Planck Florida Institute for Neuroscience.
